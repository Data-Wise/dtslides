Math & Equations

LaTeX Support

Inline math: The sample mean is \(\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i\).

Display math:

\[ f(x \mid \mu, \sigma^2) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}} \exp\left(-\frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) \]

Aligned equations:

\[ \begin{align} \hat{\beta} &= (X'X)^{-1}X'y \\ \text{Var}(\hat{\beta}) &= \sigma^2 (X'X)^{-1} \\ \hat{\sigma}^2 &= \frac{e'e}{n - p} \end{align} \]

Theme-Specific Callouts

Use divs ::: {.callout-type} for custom boxes:

Consider a study with \(n = 30\) subjects. The sample mean is \(\bar{x} = 5.2\) with \(s = 1.8\). A 95% CI for \(\mu\) is \((4.53, 5.87)\).

How does increasing the sample size affect the width of the confidence interval?

The CI width decreases at rate \(1/\sqrt{n}\), so quadrupling the sample size halves the interval width.

Citations

Inline Citations

As Box (1976) famously noted, all models are wrong but some are useful.

The bootstrap method (Efron and Tibshirani 1993) provides a nonparametric approach to inference. Exploratory data analysis (Tukey 1977) is a crucial first step. Classical foundations include (Fisher 1925) and (Neyman and Pearson 1933).

Modern methods span Bayesian analysis (Gelman et al. 2013), random forests (Breiman 2001), regularization (Tibshirani 1996), causal inference (Pearl 2009; Rubin 1974), machine learning (Hastie, Tibshirani, and Friedman 2009), and survival analysis (Cox 1972).
